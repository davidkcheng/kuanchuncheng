Problem Statement

From economies of scale, bigger order often gives lower unit price. This is a very common commercial trick that can be found in many retailer stores or restaurants. However, if you do not have a big family, buying too much same items would often cause waste, especially of the food easily expired. However, finding someone who is willing to share these items before making the purchase is not a trivial question. Also, if your friends living far from you, it takes a long time to meet with them. Therefore, it would be nice if someone living close to you is also interested in what you are buying and is willing to share the items, grocery, or food.

Based on the problem statement, my goal is to encourage the residents living in a close proximity to interact with their neighbors through developing a App to facilitate the activities of food group buying.

Sketches

After figured out the basic tasks I want to solve, I turned to find the possible apporaches towards the tasks. I use the storyboard method to brainstorm and came out many ideas for the application, including the possible personas, scenarios, and features of the applications.

Wireframe

Through storyboarding and personas, the whole information flow is built up. Then I use balsamiq mockups to build a initial wireframe of the system. At beginning, I seperated the system into three part, the item browsing, item posting, and user profile.

Based on the wireframe, I did a question-option-criteria analysis, through which I figured out it is better to have a payment management function to help users to easily manage their expenses on the group buying activities.

High-fidelity Prototype - item browsing

After choosing the “Find Good stuff” tab, users are eligible to borwse the item posted by your friends, or Someone’s post but shared to you by your friend. If you are interested in the pizza:
1. Select time/place both of you are available.
2. Select quantity you want.
3. Press "Count me In". Done.

High-fidelity Prototype - item posting

Worried about the super large pack of muffin you just bought?
1. Fill out the information
2. In the last, pick some friends and they will be notified.
3. Post It. Waiting for someone to help you!

High-fidelity Prototype - item tracking

Lets see who is joining you group. Click the post, you’ll see:
1. Who joins
2. How much they want
3. If someone is invited by your friend, you may add he/she as your friend.
You will never miss out anything.

High-fidelity Prototype - payment management

In your home page, you’ll find:
1. Your friend list
2. Who you should pay for
3. Who you should send a request for money
4. notification that you friends ask you to share something
